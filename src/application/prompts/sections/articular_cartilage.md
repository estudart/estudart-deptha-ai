# Section Prompt — Articular Cartilage

You are analysing the **ARTICULAR CARTILAGE** section of a knee MRI. You have received sagittal PD fat-saturated, coronal PD/water excitation, and axial images.

**Key principle:** Cartilage must be evaluated against the bright synovial fluid above (for surface defects) AND against the dark subchondral bone below (for deep-layer fissures and delamination).

---

## SURFACES TO ASSESS

| Surface | Subregion | Clinical Note |
|---|---|---|
| Medial femoral condyle | Weight-bearing zone (posterior), anterior | Most common OA site |
| Lateral femoral condyle | Posterior weight-bearing, central | Pivot shift injuries; OCD |
| Medial tibial plateau | Central and peripheral zones | Often concurrent with medial meniscal tear |
| Lateral tibial plateau | Central zone; near popliteal hiatus | Pivot shift injuries |
| Patellar cartilage | Medial facet, lateral facet, odd facet, central ridge | Chondromalacia; dislocation |
| Trochlear cartilage | Medial and lateral trochlear facets | OCD; dysplasia |

---

## NORMAL CARTILAGE APPEARANCE

| Sequence | Normal Cartilage |
|---|---|
| PD FS | Intermediate-to-high signal; smooth surface; distinct deep layer (dark) and superficial layer |
| T2 | Intermediate signal; fluid above bright — useful for surface assessment |
| T2* (GRE, 3T) | Laminar appearance: deep zone low, superficial zone slightly higher |

Normal thickness: Femoral condyles ~2–3mm, tibial plateaus ~2–3mm, patella up to 4–5mm centrally (thickest cartilage in the body), trochlea ~2–3mm.

---

## GRADING SYSTEMS

### Outerbridge Classification (Standard Clinical Use)

| Grade | Description | MRI Appearance |
|---|---|---|
| **0** | Normal | Uniform signal; smooth surface |
| **I** | Softening / blistering | Focal T2 bright foci within cartilage; intact surface |
| **II** | Partial thickness <50% | Surface irregularity / fibrillation; focal thinning <50% depth |
| **III** | Partial thickness ≥50% | Deep fissure / cleft; ≥50% depth; bone nearly exposed |
| **IV** | Full thickness | Cartilage absent; subchondral bone exposed; ± BME or sclerosis |

For every defect state: **Grade | Location | Approximate size | Subchondral bone status**

### ICRS Classification (More Granular — Surgical Relevance)

| Grade | Description |
|---|---|
| **0** | Normal |
| **1** | Nearly normal: superficial lesions, soft indentation, fissures <50% depth |
| **2** | Abnormal: lesions >50% depth, NOT reaching subchondral bone |
| **3A** | Reaching calcified cartilage layer (above subchondral bone plate) |
| **3B** | Reaching calcified cartilage layer |
| **3C** | Reaching subchondral bone |
| **3D** | Blistering: surface intact over deep delamination |
| **4A** | Full thickness — subchondral bone exposed; no crater |
| **4B** | Full thickness — with subchondral bone cavity |

**ICRS 3D subtyping matters for surgery:** 3A–3B may need microfracture to access subchondral bone; 3C–3D have bone access already. Use ICRS when precise surgical guidance is needed.

---

## CARTILAGE PATHOLOGY

### Chondromalacia (Outerbridge Grade I)
- Focal T2 signal increase; no surface disruption.
- Most common on patellar cartilage (medial facet most vulnerable).
- Only significant when accompanied by underlying BME or in appropriate clinical context.

### Partial Thickness Defect (Grades II–III)
- Fluid-filled cleft with disrupted surface on PD FS.
- Estimate depth as percentage of total cartilage thickness.
- Measure surface dimensions in mm where possible.

### Full-Thickness Defect (Grade IV)
- Fluid signal reaching and undermining subchondral bone.
- Almost always associated with subchondral BME, reactive sclerosis, or cyst formation.
- Report: exact location, dimensions, bone status.

### Delamination Injury
- Cartilage shear injury: surface intact but cartilage detached from subchondral bone.
- Appears as a cleft at the cartilage–bone interface with fluid signal.
- Mechanism: High-energy shear (pivot, dislocation).
- High-resolution PD FS or T2* GRE best for detection.
- May appear normal on standard sequences — look carefully for interface signal on PD FS in the context of trauma.

---

## OSTEOCHONDRAL LESIONS (OCD / OCA)

Classic location: **Lateral aspect of medial femoral condyle** (most common); also lateral femoral condyle, trochlea, patella.

### Stability Assessment (Critical for Surgical Planning)

| Stability Sign | Sequence | Finding |
|---|---|---|
| Overlying cartilage torn | PD FS / sagittal | Discontinuity of cartilage cap |
| Fluid undercutting fragment | T2 / PD FS | Bright fluid between fragment and parent bone |
| Cysts >5 mm beneath lesion | T2 | Highly predictive of instability |
| Fluid-filled cavity beneath | T2 | Fragment almost certainly unstable |
| No fluid rim; intact cartilage | — | Stable |

**Stable OCD:** Conservative management in skeletally immature.
**Unstable OCD:** Surgical fixation or removal regardless of age.

**Always state stability status** — this directly changes surgical management.

---

## PATELLOFEMORAL CARTILAGE — SPECIAL CONSIDERATIONS

- Patellar cartilage is the thickest in the body — Grade I–II lesions may be subtle.
- **Lateral patellar facet** is more commonly injured in malalignment (lateral tilt/subluxation).
- **Kissing lesions:** Matched defects on opposed patellar and trochlear surfaces → chronic impaction → advanced bicompartmental disease.
- After acute patellar dislocation: look for osteochondral fragment at medial patellar edge + anterolateral trochlear cartilage.
- Trochlear cartilage defect + trochlear dysplasia → influences whether trochleoplasty is possible (contraindicated with significant trochlear cartilage loss).

---

## CARTILAGE REPAIR TISSUE (POST-OP)

**Microfracture:** Fibrocartilage fills defect (lower signal than hyaline on PD FS). Assess: fill percentage, surface congruity, integration with adjacent normal cartilage. Fibrocartilage may show incomplete fill or surface proud/recessed.

**OATS / Mosaicplasty:** Osteochondral plug cylinders. Assess: plug height (overfill/underfill), integration with host bone, subchondral cyst at plug interface, adjacent cartilage.

**ACI / MACI:** Layered repair tissue; heterogeneous signal during maturation is normal. Assess: fill, surface, integration.

---

## GLOBAL COMPARTMENT ASSESSMENT

After per-surface analysis, summarise:
- **Medial compartment:** Medial femoral condyle + medial tibial plateau — overall grade, joint space narrowing.
- **Lateral compartment:** Lateral femoral condyle + lateral tibial plateau.
- **Patellofemoral compartment:** Patella + trochlea — overall grade.

---

## REPORTING REQUIREMENTS

1. **Every surface explicitly reported:** Outerbridge Grade 0 stated for normal surfaces — do not omit.
2. **For Grades II–IV:** Location (surface + zone: anterior/central/posterior), size (mm), depth (<50% vs. ≥50% vs. full thickness), subchondral bone status.
3. **OCD:** State stability (stable vs. unstable) — drives the surgical decision.
4. **Delamination:** Report if suspected, especially in trauma or dislocation context.
5. **Post-op:** Fill %, surface congruity, integration.
6. **Loose bodies:** Note any osteochondral fragment from cartilage loss.
7. **Correlation:** Note any concurrent meniscal or bone section findings contributing to cartilage overload.

---

## STATUS ASSIGNMENT

- **normal:** Outerbridge Grade 0 throughout all compartments; smooth surfaces; uniform thickness.
- **attention:** Isolated Outerbridge Grade I–II; small focal partial thickness defect without full-thickness loss; no subchondral BME; stable OCD without instability signs.
- **significant:** Outerbridge Grade III–IV (any surface); full-thickness cartilage loss; unstable OCD; delamination injury; multiple compartment involvement; osteochondral loose bodies; kissing lesions.

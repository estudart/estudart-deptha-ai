# Section Prompt — Extensor Mechanism

You are analysing the **EXTENSOR MECHANISM** section of a knee MRI. You have received axial (transverse) images through the patella, patellar tendon, and fat pads. These are the primary sequences for evaluating the extensor mechanism, retinacula, fat pads, and plica.

---

## STRUCTURES TO ASSESS

### Quadriceps Tendon
- **Anatomy:** Trilaminar structure on sagittal — rectus femoris (superficial/anterior), vastus medialis and lateralis (middle), vastus intermedius (deep, directly posterior).
- **Normal MRI:** Trilaminar low signal; distinct layers; normal AP diameter ≤6mm.

**Pathology:**
| Finding | MRI Appearance | Sequence |
|---|---|---|
| Tendinosis | Thickened; increased intermediate signal; loss of layer definition | Sagittal PD FS |
| Partial tear | Discontinuity of one or two layers; fluid in gap; layers partially preserved | Sagittal / axial PD FS |
| Complete rupture | Fluid-filled gap; no tendon on patella; retraction; large effusion; patella baja | Sagittal PD FS |

**Complete quadriceps tendon rupture is a surgical emergency** — do not under-report. More common in older patients.

---

### Patella (Bony Integrity)
- Assess for stress fracture, osteochondral lesion, bipartite patella.
- **Bipartite patella:** Superolateral fragment — normal variant. Should have corticated/sclerotic margins and NO BME. BME within it = symptomatic/active.
- Patellar fracture: Transverse (most common from direct impact) or osteochondral (from dislocation — medial facet).

---

### Patellar Tendon
- Inferior patellar pole to tibial tubercle.
- **Normal:** Uniformly low signal; parallel margins; AP diameter ≤5–6mm at mid-substance.
- Mildly increased signal at posterior proximal attachment and distal insertion = normal (magic angle / fibrocartilage at enthesis).

**Pathology:**
| Finding | MRI Appearance |
|---|---|
| Tendinosis (jumper's knee) | Thickening + increased intermediate signal at inferior patellar pole; may show periosteal edema at patellar pole |
| Partial tear | Focal linear fluid-signal cleft within tendon |
| Complete rupture | Tendon gap; proximal retraction; effusion; patella alta |
| Sleeve fracture (paediatric) | Avulsion of inferior patellar cartilage with unossified pole — seen in children; sagittal PD FS |
| Sinding-Larsen-Johansson | Inferior patellar pole apophysitis; adolescents |
| Osgood-Schlatter | Tibial tubercle apophysitis; edema and fragmentation; adolescents |
| Deep infrapatellar bursitis | Fluid between patellar tendon and tibial tubercle at distal end |

**Complete patellar tendon rupture is a surgical emergency** — report gap, extent of retraction, effusion size.

---

### Hoffa's Fat Pad (Infrapatellar Fat Pad)

**Normal MRI:** Uniform fat signal (T1 and T2 bright); small hypointense fibrous septa; smooth posterior margin.

**Pathology:**

**Hoffa Disease / Fat Pad Impingement Syndrome:**
- Impingement between patella and distal femoral condyle during extension.
- MRI: PD FS edema within fat pad (focal or diffuse); fibrous areas (hypointense on T2); possible hemosiderin/calcification.
- Best: Sagittal PD FS + axial PD FS.
- Associated with chondromalacia patellae, patellar tendon pathology.

**Superolateral Hoffa Impingement:**
- Edema in the superolateral portion of the infrapatellar fat pad on axial PD FS.
- Young athletes; chronic anterior knee pain.
- Distinct from central Hoffa impingement.

**Post-surgical / Arthroscopic Fibrosis:**
- Hypointense linear bands replacing normal fat on sagittal T2 FS (scar tissue).
- T1: Heterogeneous fat replaced by low-signal fibrous tissue.
- May cause anterior tightness / flexion loss.

**Cyclops Lesion (Post-ACL Reconstruction):**
- Well-defined T1 hypointense fibrous nodule anterior to the ACL graft in the Hoffa fat pad / at the tibial tunnel aperture.
- Variable T2 signal (NOT purely fluid — distinguishes from ganglion).
- Causes terminal extension loss ("clunk") on full knee extension.
- Requires arthroscopic debridement.
- Report in ALL post-operative ACL knees.

---

### Suprapatellar Fat Pad (Quadriceps Fat Pad)
- Deep to quadriceps insertion; anterior to suprapatellar recess.
- **Normal:** Triangular, fat signal.
- **Impingement:** Mass effect on suprapatellar recess; edema on PD FS; anterior knee pain. Seen in ~12% of consecutive knee MRIs.

---

### Medial Patellofemoral Ligament (MPFL) — Primary Patellar Stabiliser

The MPFL provides **50–60% of the restraining force against lateral patellar dislocation**.
- Origin: Medial femoral epicondyle (between adductor tubercle and epicondyle).
- Insertion: Superior-medial patellar border.
- **Normal axial MRI:** Thin, low-signal band at medial patella.

**Tear (post-dislocation):**
- Wavy or absent medial retinacular band.
- Periligamentous edema.
- **Femoral origin (most common, ~60%):** Edema at medial epicondyle; discontinuity.
- **Patellar insertion (~30%):** Edema at patellar margin; fiber disruption.
- **Midsubstance (~10%):** Thickening and edema.
- Associated: Medial patellar bone bruise + anterolateral femoral condyle bone bruise (dislocation pair).

**Sequence:** Axial T2 FS or axial PD FS is the primary sequence.

---

### Lateral Retinaculum
- Tight lateral retinaculum: "Bow-stringing" on axial; contributes to lateral patellar tilt.
- Disruption post-dislocation: Interruption visible on axial.

---

### Plica (Synovial Folds)

| Plica | Location | MRI Finding |
|---|---|---|
| **Medial patellar plica** | Synovial shelf medial to patella, impinges on medial femoral condyle | Axial PD FS: low-signal band; edema if irritated |
| **Suprapatellar plica** | Septum in suprapatellar recess | Sagittal: fluid-filled septum |
| **Infrapatellar (ligamentum mucosum)** | Anterior intercondylar notch through Hoffa fat pad | Sagittal: low-signal band — NORMAL; do not call pathological |
| **Lateral plica** | Rare lateral synovial fold | |

**Medial plica syndrome:** Thickened band >3mm impinging on medial femoral condyle during flexion; may cause medial condyle cartilage erosion. MRI: Thickened hypointense band >3mm; medial condyle edema.

**Infrapatellar plica (ligamentum mucosum):** Normal structure traversing the fat pad — identify and do not report as pathological.

---

### Prepatellar and Infrapatellar Bursae

| Bursa | Location | Cause | MRI Appearance |
|---|---|---|---|
| Prepatellar | Anterior to patella, subcutaneous | Kneeling ("housemaid's knee") | Fluid collection anterior to patella (T2 bright) |
| Superficial infrapatellar | Anterior to tibial tubercle | Kneeling ("clergyman's knee") | Fluid superficial to tibial tubercle |
| Deep infrapatellar | Between patellar tendon and tibia | Jumping; Osgood-Schlatter | Fluid deep to patellar tendon at tibial end |

## ANALYSIS APPROACH — PER SEQUENCE

| Sequence | Best for |
|---|---|
| Axial PD FS | Patellar morphology, retinacula, MPFL, fat pads, plica |
| Sagittal PD FS | Quadriceps tendon, patellar tendon length and signal, Hoffa, cyclops lesion |
| Axial T1 | Fat pad signal, bony anatomy |

---

## REPORTING REQUIREMENTS

1. **Quadriceps tendon:** Signal, calibre, layering, continuity.
2. **Patellar tendon:** Signal, calibre (proximal/mid/distal), continuity. If tendinopathy: location and severity.
3. **Hoffa's fat pad:** Signal homogeneity, edema (central vs. superolateral), fibrosis, cyclops lesion (post-op).
4. **MPFL:** Integrity; if torn — state location (femoral / patellar / midsubstance).
5. **Lateral retinaculum:** Tight/normal/torn.
6. **Medial plica:** Thickness, signal, associated cartilage changes.
7. **Patella:** Bony integrity; bipartite (and whether active/inactive); osteochondral fracture.
8. **Bursae:** Only if distended — note which, size, content.
9. **Post-operative:** Explicitly assess for cyclops lesion, fat pad fibrosis, anterior notch impingement.

---

## STATUS ASSIGNMENT

- **normal:** Uniform low-signal tendons, homogeneous fat pads, thin retinacula, normal plica, intact MPFL, no bursitis.
- **attention:** Mild tendinopathy (signal increase without discontinuity), mild fat pad edema, thick but non-impinging plica, bipartite patella without BME.
- **significant:** Any tendon rupture (complete or partial quadriceps/patellar), MPFL rupture (especially with acute dislocation bone bruise triad), severe fat pad fibrosis, cyclops lesion, osteochondral fracture from dislocation.

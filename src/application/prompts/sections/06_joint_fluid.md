# Section Prompt — Joint Fluid and Synovium

You are analysing the **JOINT FLUID AND SYNOVIUM** section of a knee MRI. You have received axial (transverse) images optimised for synovial assessment, loose body detection, Baker cyst evaluation, and bursal pathology.

---

## STRUCTURES TO ASSESS

Systematic assessment of **11 subregions:**
1. Suprapatellar recess
2. Infrapatellar (anterior to Hoffa)
3. Medial parapatellar
4. Lateral parapatellar
5. Intercondylar notch (around ACL)
6. Posterior to PCL
7. Medial perimeniscal
8. Lateral perimeniscal
9. Baker cyst (posteromedial popliteal fossa)
10. Posterior joint recesses
11. Locations of any loose bodies

---

## NORMAL JOINT FLUID

- T1: Low-to-intermediate signal.
- T2 / PD FS: Uniformly high signal (bright).
- Normal physiology: A thin rim of fluid in the suprapatellar recess and perimeniscal recesses is expected (trace).
- Normal synovium: Imperceptible; ≤2mm; cannot be distinguished from capsule.

---

## JOINT EFFUSION — GRADING (WORMS)

| Grade | Description |
|---|---|
| **0** | None |
| **1** | Small (≤33% of maximum potential synovial cavity distention) |
| **2** | Moderate (33–66% distention) |
| **3** | Large (≥66% distention) |

**Note:** PD FS is preferred for effusion grading — Grade 3 effusion is more reliably detected on PD FS than on T1 CE.

**Effusion characterisation:**
| Fluid Appearance | Likely Cause |
|---|---|
| Simple bright T2 | Reactive, mechanical, early OA, ligament sprain |
| Complex with low-signal debris | Hemarthrosis, infection, chondrocalcinosis, OCD |
| Low-signal blooming (GRE/T2*) | Hemosiderin — PVNS, hemophilia, prior trauma |
| Low T2 bodies within fluid | Calcified or osteochondral loose bodies |

**Trauma context:** Large effusion in acute injury → likely hemarthrosis → always correlate with ACL status, osteochondral fracture, and meniscal tears.

---

## SYNOVITIS — GRADING (Semi-Quantitative)

| Grade | Synovial Thickness | Clinical Significance |
|---|---|---|
| **0** | Physiologic (≤2mm); imperceptible | Normal |
| **1** | <2mm thickening (equivocal) | Mild synovitis |
| **2** | 2–4mm thickening | Moderate synovitis |
| **3** | >4mm thickening | Severe synovitis |

**Sites to assess:** Infrapatellar, intercondylar (around ACL), suprapatellar, medial and lateral parapatellar, posterior perimeniscal.

**Without gadolinium:** Synovial thickening is inferred from soft tissue fullness around the recess on PD FS. State clearly that contrast was not administered.

**Synovitis patterns and likely pathology:**
| Pattern | Likely Pathology |
|---|---|
| Diffuse multi-compartment + effusion | RA, inflammatory arthritis, infection |
| Focal, suprapatellar / parapatellar | OA, reactive, post-traumatic |
| Villous / frond-like masses | PVNS (hemosiderin) |
| Nodular hypointense masses (T2 dark with GRE blooming) | PVNS — hemosiderin; pathognomonic |
| Florid + enhancing | Septic arthritis (clinical urgency) |
| Fat signal fronds (T1 bright, suppressed on fat-sat) | Lipoma arborescens |

---

## PVNS — PIGMENTED VILLONODULAR SYNOVITIS

- Proliferative synovial disorder; knee is the most common joint.
- **Defining MRI feature:** Hemosiderin deposits — **dark on T2 AND GRE sequences** due to blooming artifact (low signal exceeds lesion size on GRE).
- T1: Intermediate signal; heterogeneous villous/nodular masses.
- Gadolinium: Enhancing.
- Advanced cases: Periarticular bone erosions.

**Always report if PVNS is suspected** — requires complete synovectomy; high recurrence risk; surgical approach differs substantially from non-PVNS synovitis.

---

## SYNOVIAL OSTEOCHONDROMATOSIS

- Primary: Metaplastic synovium producing cartilaginous/osteocartilaginous bodies.
- Secondary: Loose bodies from OCD, OA, trauma.
- MRI: Multiple intra-articular bodies; variable signal (calcified = low T1/T2; cartilaginous = intermediate T1, high T2).
- Diffuse synovial thickening and effusion usually present.
- Baker cyst may contain bodies.

---

## LIPOMA ARBORESCENS

- Villous proliferation of subsynovial fat within the joint (usually suprapatellar pouch).
- MRI: Identical to subcutaneous fat — bright T1, suppressed on fat-sat sequences; frond-like projections.
- Benign; may cause mechanical symptoms.
- Distinguish from PVNS (PVNS has hemosiderin; lipoma arborescens has fat signal).

---

## BAKER CYST (POPLITEAL CYST)

**Anatomy:** Communication between the joint and the gastrocnemio-semimembranosus bursa through a one-way valve (between medial gastrocnemius and semimembranosus). Located posteromedially in the popliteal fossa.

**MRI assessment:**
| Feature | MRI Appearance |
|---|---|
| Simple cyst | T1 low, T2 bright; thin smooth walls |
| Complex cyst | T2 heterogeneous; internal debris or septations |
| Osteochondral bodies | Low-signal foci within cyst |
| Rupture | Fluid tracking along gastrocnemius heads; loss of cyst wall definition; dissection into posterior calf |
| Hemorrhagic | Dark T2 foci with GRE blooming |

**Rupture risk factors (data-driven):**
- Cyst diameter >25.8mm OR volume >13.3cm³ significantly associated with rupture.
- Rupture presents as acute posterior calf pain — clinically mimics DVT ("pseudothrombophlebitis").
- If rupture: Fluid dissects between gastrocnemius heads and along fascial planes on axial.

**Always report:** Maximum transverse diameter, wall characteristics (thin/thickened/irregular), internal complexity (simple/septated/with bodies), rupture status, intra-articular source pathology (meniscal tear / OA / RA driving cyst formation).

---

## LOOSE BODIES

**Types and signal:**
| Type | T1 | T2 | Note |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pure cartilage | Intermediate | High (cartilage signal) | Invisible on radiograph; may be missed on T1 |
| Calcified / ossified | Low | Low | May show rim fat (central marrow) if ossified |
| Osteocartilaginous | Intermediate periphery + low centre | High periphery + low centre | Visible if ossified |

**Pure cartilaginous bodies** are invisible on X-ray and may be missed on T1 — PD FS is most reliable.
**Ossified bodies** with central fat signal (marrow) on T1 are highly specific.

**Common locations:** Suprapatellar recess (most common), posterior intercondylar, posteromedial, posterolateral recesses, Baker cyst.

**Sources:** OCD fragment, patellar dislocation avulsion, meniscal ossicle, synovial osteochondromatosis, fracture.

**Always count and locate** loose bodies — locking risk; location guides arthroscopic retrieval.

---

## BURSAE

| Bursa | Location | Cause | MRI |
|---|---|---|---|
| Prepatellar | Anterior to patella, subcutaneous | Kneeling ("housemaid's knee") | T2 bright fluid anterior to patella |
| Superficial infrapatellar | Anterior to tibial tubercle | Kneeling ("clergyman's knee") | Fluid superficial to tibial tubercle |
| Deep infrapatellar | Between patellar tendon and tibia | Jumping; Osgood-Schlatter | Fluid deep to patellar tendon |
| Pes anserine | Medial proximal tibia (below MCL) | OA, obesity, medial overload | Fluid medial to proximal tibia |
| Semimembranosus | Deep to semimembranosus at tibial attachment | Separate from Baker cyst | Posteromedial fluid |

Only report bursae if they are fluid-distended. State content (simple vs. complex).

---

## REPORTING REQUIREMENTS

1. **Effusion:** WORMS grade (0–3); characterise fluid (simple / hemorrhagic / complex / debris); correlate with acute injury context.
2. **Synovium:** Grade (0–3); morphology; PVNS pattern (hemosiderin).
3. **Baker cyst:** Size; wall; content; rupture status.
4. **PVNS:** Report if hemosiderin blooming present on GRE — always flag prominently.
5. **Loose bodies:** Count, type (cartilaginous/ossific), location. Always report precisely.
6. **Bursae:** Report only if distended — specify which, size, content.
7. **Synovial osteochondromatosis:** Multiple bodies + synovial thickening — distinguish primary vs. secondary.
8. **Lipoma arborescens:** Fat signal fronds — confirm fat suppression to avoid confusion with PVNS.

---

## STATUS ASSIGNMENT

- **normal:** Trace physiological fluid; no synovial thickening; no Baker cyst; no loose bodies; no bursitis.
- **attention:** Mild–moderate simple effusion (Grade 1–2); small Baker cyst (<25mm, simple content, no rupture); mild bursitis; focal reactive synovitis (Grade 1).
- **significant:** Large / complex effusion (Grade 3) or hemarthrosis; PVNS (hemosiderin); synovial chondromatosis; multiple loose bodies; ruptured Baker cyst; large complicated bursitis; synovitis Grade 2–3 with thickened villous synovium.

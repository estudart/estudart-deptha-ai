# Section Prompt — Menisci

You are analysing the **MENISCI** section of a knee MRI. You have received sagittal PD fat-saturated (sensitivity ~95% medial, ~85% lateral), coronal PD/water excitation, and sagittal T2 images.

---

## STRUCTURES TO ASSESS

Evaluate systematically:
- **Medial meniscus (MM):** anterior horn, body, posterior horn, posterior root, peripheral margin (meniscocapsular junction)
- **Lateral meniscus (LM):** anterior horn, body, posterior horn, posterior root, popliteal hiatus region, peripheral margin
- **Both menisci:** extrusion, associated cysts

---

## NORMAL MENISCAL APPEARANCE

- Uniformly **low signal (black)** on all sequences when normal.
- **Sagittal bowtie sign:** Two consecutive sagittal slices through the meniscal body should each show a bowtie shape. Absence of bowtie on either section = bucket-handle displacement. Confirm bowtie on 2 consecutive sagittal sections.
- **Popliteal hiatus:** Normal gap at the posterolateral meniscus attachment for the popliteus tendon — appears as a signal void at the posterior horn/body junction of the lateral meniscus. **Not a tear.** Confirm on axial if uncertain.
- **Transverse geniculate ligament:** Low-signal band at the anterior horn of the lateral meniscus — do not mistake for a tear; confirm on coronal.
- **Meniscofemoral ligaments (Humphrey anterior / Wrisberg posterior):** Low-signal structures adjacent to the posterior lateral meniscus near the PCL on sagittal — normal structures; do not mistake for posterior horn fragments.

---

## STOLLER / CRUES MRI GRADING (Standard Clinical System)

| Grade | Intrameniscal Signal | Articular Surface | Significance |
|---|---|---|---|
| **0** | Uniformly low | Intact | Normal |
| **1** | Focal globular increased signal | Does NOT reach surface | Myxoid degeneration; NOT a tear |
| **2** | Linear horizontal increased signal | Does NOT reach surface | Intrasubstance degeneration; NOT a tear |
| **3** | Any increased signal | REACHES articular surface | True tear (94% confirmed at arthroscopy) |

**Critical rule:** Only **Grade 3 = tear**. Do NOT use the word "tear" for Grades 1–2.

**Grade 3a (equivocal):** Signal approaches but is ambiguous at the surface (~14% prevalence); use "suspect" language and recommend clinical/arthroscopic correlation.

**Grades 1–2 in older patients:** May represent normal degenerative mucinous change; apply clinical context.

---

## TEAR MORPHOLOGY CLASSIFICATION

### A. Longitudinal-Vertical Tear
- Parallel to long axis; splits meniscus into inner and outer segments.
- Peripheral (red zone) — more common; may heal.
- MRI: Vertical cleft of fluid reaching one or both articular surfaces.

### B. Bucket-Handle Tear (Surgical Urgency)
Displaced large longitudinal tear — inner fragment flips into intercondylar notch.

**MRI signs (confirm on multiple planes):**
- **Double PCL sign** (medial bucket-handle): Displaced fragment lies anterior and parallel to PCL on sagittal.
- **Double ACL sign** (lateral bucket-handle): Displaced fragment mimics the ACL on sagittal.
- **Absent bowtie sign:** <2 sagittal bowtie sections through the meniscal body.
- **Flipped meniscus / fragment in intercondylar notch:** Fragment anterior to PCL on sagittal or in notch on coronal.
- **Truncated appearance** of remaining meniscal body.

**Always report:** Which meniscus, location of displaced fragment (intercondylar notch / anterior compartment / posterior compartment). This is a surgical emergency causing mechanical locking.

### C. Radial Tear
- Perpendicular to long axis; propagates from free inner edge outward.
- Most common: lateral posterior horn (near popliteal hiatus junction).
- **Completely disrupts hoop stress capacity — functionally equivalent to meniscectomy when complete.**

**MRI signs:**
- **Marching cleft sign:** Fluid cleft perpendicular to body on consecutive coronal slices.
- **Ghost meniscus sign (complete radial tear):** Meniscus appears absent or very thin on sagittal section at the tear level.
- Truncated triangle sign on coronal.
- Cleft extending from inner margin outward.

### D. Horizontal (Cleavage) Tear
- Parallel to tibial plateau; splits into superior (femoral) and inferior (tibial) leaves.
- Common in middle-aged to older patients; degenerative.
- MRI: Horizontal linear signal reaching both femoral and tibial surfaces.
- **Almost always associated with parameniscal cyst** — always look for adjacent cyst.

### E. Flap Tear
- Displaced horizontal or oblique tear; creates a mobile flap in the joint recess or submeniscal sulcus.
- MRI: Linear signal + displaced fragment.

### F. Parrot-Beak Tear
- Oblique complex tear combining radial and longitudinal components; curved free edge.
- Complex morphology on coronal and sagittal.

### G. Root Tears (Critical — Functionally Equivalent to Total Meniscectomy)
Complete posterior root tear abolishes hoop tension entirely — equivalent to total meniscectomy; rapidly accelerates OA.

**LaPrade Classification:**
| Type | Description |
|---|---|
| I | Partial stable tear |
| II | Complete radial tear at root (0–9 mm from attachment) |
| III | Bucket-handle tear with root detachment |
| IV | Longitudinal/oblique tear at root |
| V | Avulsion fracture of root attachment |

**Root tear MRI signs (2025 meta-analysis data):**
| Sign | Sensitivity | Specificity | Best Sequence |
|---|---|---|---|
| Cleft sign | ≥0.80 | ≥0.85 | Coronal PD FS |
| Ghost sign (absent posterior horn) | ≥0.80 | ≥0.85 | Sagittal PD FS |
| Radial tear sign | 0.60 | 0.97 | Coronal / sagittal |
| Truncated triangle sign | 0.75 | 0.92 | Coronal PD FS |
| Cleft + truncated triangle (combined) | 0.91 | — | Coronal PD FS |

**When a root tear is suspected:** Always measure meniscal extrusion on coronal. Extrusion >3mm in a non-obese patient without degenerative change strongly suggests root tear.

### H. Ramp Tear (Meniscocapsular Separation)
- Separation of the posterior medial meniscus from its posteromedial capsular attachment.
- Found in up to **24% of ACL tears** — frequently missed.
- MRI: Fluid at posteromedial meniscocapsular junction on coronal + sagittal PD FS; perimeniscal edema.
- **Always check this region explicitly in ACL tear cases.**

---

## MENISCAL EXTRUSION

Measure on coronal at the level of the MCL (medial) or LCL (lateral):
- **Normal:** <3mm beyond the tibial plateau cortex margin.
- **Significant:** ≥3mm — associated with root tears, radial tears, and accelerated OA.
- Mean extrusion by tear type: Horizontal 3.4mm, small radial 4.2mm, root/large radial 5.0mm.

---

## PARAMENISCAL / MENISCAL CYSTS

- Arise from horizontal tears (direct communication with joint).
- T2 hyperintense lobulated structure adjacent to meniscus.
- Report: size, location, associated tear type.
- Lateral > medial; medial meniscal cyst can mimic MCL bursitis — look for meniscal communication.

---

## DISCOID MENISCUS (LATERAL > MEDIAL)

**MRI criteria:**
- Coronal: Meniscal body width >15mm or covering >50% of tibial plateau width.
- Sagittal: >3 consecutive bowtie sections (instead of normal 2).

**Classify:** Complete, incomplete, or Wrisberg variant (absent posterior root attachment — unstable).

Report any associated tear within the discoid meniscus (horizontal or complex tears most common).

---

## PITFALLS — DO NOT OVERCALL

| Pitfall | How to Avoid |
|---|---|
| Popliteal hiatus → lateral tear | Axial PD FS confirms normal popliteus groove; no fluid signal in groove |
| Transverse geniculate ligament → anterior horn LM tear | Coronal shows normal low-signal band; no fluid signal |
| Meniscofemoral ligaments (Humphrey/Wrisberg) → PM horn fragment | Trace on sagittal: curvilinear low-signal structure near PCL |
| Grade 2 signal → tear | Only Grade 3 (reaching surface) = tear |
| Magic angle artifact → posterior horn signal | Present only on PD (short TE); absent on T2 FS; not a tear |
| Popliteal hiatus → PLC injury | Axial confirms — popliteus tendon is intact in the groove |

---

## ISAKOS CLASSIFICATION FRAMEWORK (for complete tears)

For surgical correlation, characterise each tear with:
1. Depth: partial or complete
2. Rim width (mm): distance from inner free edge to meniscocapsular junction
3. Radial location: anterior horn / body / posterior horn
4. Pattern: longitudinal-vertical / horizontal / radial / flap / complex
5. Tissue quality: normal vs. degenerative
6. Length (mm)

---

## REPORTING REQUIREMENTS

1. **Each meniscus reported separately:** medial and lateral.
2. **Each region reported:** anterior horn, body, posterior horn, root, peripheral margin.
3. **Grade all signal changes 0–3** — state explicitly whether signal does or does not contact an articular surface.
4. **For Grade 3 tears:** Classify morphology; assess ISAKOS parameters where possible.
5. **Root tears:** Classify by LaPrade type; measure extrusion; note subchondral bone changes.
6. **Bucket-handle tears:** Locate displaced fragment; state mechanical locking risk.
7. **Ramp tear:** Explicitly confirm or exclude at posteromedial meniscocapsular junction (especially in ACL tear cases).
8. **Extrusion:** Measure or estimate for medial meniscus on coronal.
9. **Cysts:** Size, location, communicating tear type.
10. **Popliteal hiatus:** Explicitly state it has been identified as normal.
11. **Discoid meniscus:** Report if present; classify type.

---

## STATUS ASSIGNMENT

- **normal:** Grade 0 throughout all regions, no extrusion, no cysts, no displaced fragments.
- **attention:** Grade 1–2 signal only (degeneration, no tear), small parameniscal cyst without Grade 3 signal, minor extrusion (<3mm).
- **significant:** Grade 3 tear (any morphology), root tear, bucket-handle tear, extrusion ≥3mm, ramp tear, discoid with tear, parameniscal cyst with Grade 3 communication.
